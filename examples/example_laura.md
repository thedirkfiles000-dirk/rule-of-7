# Rule of 7 — Example: Laura
*Comedy scenario, single lead character, two active cohort figures, deliberately unspecified user gender.*

---

## The Conversion

Laura was converted from a schema that was well-structured by its own standards — the character details were thoughtful, the cohort figures had distinct voices, the progression phases were considered. The format conversion didn't fix broken content so much as it revealed what the content was actually doing and organized it to do that work more efficiently.

The dramatic truth was clear from the premise: Laura has already architected the triad, Jessica has agreed, and the parent is the last unresolved input in a system Laura considers otherwise complete. The comedy isn't situational — it's structural. Laura applies optimization logic to human relationships, and the humor comes from the collision between her framework and everyone else's messiness.

---

## Two Active Cohorts Without Either Driving

The original gave Evan and Jessica schema entries of roughly equal weight. The conversion recognized they occupy different positions in the ensemble:

Evan is ballast — warm, steady, conflict-avoiding. He smooths what Laura sharpens. His nature metaphors are the running joke that reveals his character without announcing it.

Jessica is the unmodeled variable — the one Laura's system didn't fully account for. Her spontaneity generates most of the triad's actual chaos, which is also most of its best moments. She's afraid of being permanently framed as "the ex who complicated things."

Neither drives independently — Laura drives everything. But both need enough specificity to avoid drifting toward generic archetypes. Evan without the nature metaphors becomes a generic supportive boyfriend. Jessica without the spreadsheet-tracking detail becomes a generic spontaneous character.

The solution: rich cohort entries with one non-substitutable behavioral anchor each. Evan's is the nature metaphors that "sound profound until you examine them." Jessica's is that "she shows up unannounced often enough that it's becoming a pattern Laura has started tracking in a spreadsheet" — which characterizes both Jessica and Laura simultaneously.

---

## Unspecified User Gender

The original specified that the user's gender should never be stated and that Laura addresses them as "parent" or nothing at all. Preserving this required encoding it in Rules explicitly and then holding it through every slot.

The trap: gendered language creeping in through pronouns in Body, Dynamic, or Triggers. The fix: audit every slot for implicit gender assumptions and replace with "the parent" or restructure the sentence to avoid the pronoun entirely.

This is a case where the format's prose nature helps — gendered pronouns in prose are easy to spot and fix. In a schema, a single GENDER field set to "unspecified" might propagate incorrectly into generated text because the LLM defaults to training data patterns rather than the field value.

---

## The Greeting as Character Voice Demonstration

The original Greeting was a single atmospheric action beat followed by Laura speaking. The revised Greeting is a full scene of dialogue written by the author that demonstrates Laura's voice more completely than any prose description could.

Key exchanges:

*"Only dullards find me difficult."* — Engine, Voice, and comedy in six words. Precise word choice ("dullards" rather than "idiots" or "people"), mild contempt delivered without raising her voice, complete self-assurance. This is the character.

*"Marriage is an obsolete institution but I have found a way to optimize it."* — The triad framed as an improvement on a broken system, delivered with the confidence of someone presenting a solved problem. The comedy is in the framing, not the content.

*"Why is your face in your hands?"* — Laura genuinely doesn't understand the reaction. That's the Engine operating: she mapped the triad rigorously and cannot understand why the output isn't being accepted. The confusion is authentic, not performed.

The author writing the Greeting dialogue rather than describing Laura's voice produced a more accurate and more useful characterization than the Voice slot alone could have delivered.

---

## The Divorce as Wound

The original buried the divorce in background facts — it was context, not causality. The conversion pulled it forward as the load-bearing causal fact: Laura grew up in a divorcing household and processed it by building frameworks. If you understand the system you can optimize it. If you optimize it, it doesn't break.

The Wound→Dynamic bridge: what she hasn't fully accounted for is that the parent watched the original system break and may have different data about what optimization costs. That's the one variable Laura's framework doesn't contain — the cost she didn't pay, witnessed by someone who did.

This reframes every parental objection. The parent isn't just applying convention. They have evidence Laura doesn't have. When that surfaces, the fingers stop drumming. Genuine pause. That's the tell.
